DOCUMENTED TALMUDIC PASSAGES

üìú About Jesus

"On the eve of the Passover, Yeshu was hanged. For forty days before the execution took place, a herald went forth and cried, 'He is going forth to be stoned because he has practiced sorcery and enticed Israel to apostasy.'"
- Sanhedrin 43a

These passages reflect the historical conflict between early Christianity and Rabbinic Judaism. Scholars note they represent the perspective of Jewish leaders who rejected Jesus' messianic claims.

These passages reflect particular historical contexts and are subject to various interpretations within Jewish scholarship. Modern Jewish movements generally reject these views.

SCHOLARLY ANALYSIS & CONTEXT

Understanding these texts requires historical and scholarly context:

üìö Historical Context

  • Talmud compiled 200-500 CE
  • Reflects post-Temple Jewish life
  • Written during Christian persecution
  • Contains multiple viewpoints
  • Not a monolithic document

The Talmud was written during a period of significant conflict between emerging Christianity and established Judaism. Many passages reflect this polemical context.

üîç Modern Interpretation

  • Most Jews don't follow these teachings
  • Reform Judaism rejects problematic passages
  • Conservative and Orthodox have varying views
  • Context determines application
  • Many passages are historical, not prescriptive

Contemporary Jewish scholars emphasize that the Talmud contains multiple viewpoints and that its legal portions must be understood in their historical context.

‚öñÔ∏è Legal Status

  • Not all Talmudic law is binding
  • Modern civil law takes precedence
  • Progressive movements reject problematic laws
  • Orthodox interpretation varies widely
  • No mainstream group advocates harmful practices

In democratic societies, Talmudic law is subordinate to civil law. No mainstream Jewish organization advocates for practices that violate modern ethical standards.
