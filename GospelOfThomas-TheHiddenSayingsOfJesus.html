The Lost Teachings of Jesus

What is the Gospel of Thomas?

Discovered in Nag Hammadi, Egypt in 1945, this collection of 114 sayings of Jesus predates the canonical gospels and presents a radically different Christianity.

Why Was It Hidden?

The early Church suppressed Thomas because it emphasized direct spiritual knowledge over institutional religion and contained teachings that threatened Church authority.

The Colombian Emerald Connection

Just as Colombia's emeralds are precious hidden treasures, the Gospel of Thomas contains precious hidden spiritual treasures.

The Shocking Truth

Thomas presents Jesus not as a savior to be worshipped, but as a spiritual guide who reveals that divinity is within each person. This was considered too dangerous for the masses.

Revolutionary Sayings That Change Everything

Saying 3: The Kingdom Within

Saying 3

"The kingdom is inside of you, and it is outside of you. When you come to know yourselves, then you will become known, and you will realize that it is you who are the sons of the living father. But if you will not know yourselves, you dwell in poverty and it is you who are that poverty."

Why This Was Suppressed:

This eliminates the need for priests, churches, and sacraments - everyone has direct access to divinity through self-knowledge.

Saying 70: The Inner Fire

Saying 70

"If you bring forth what is within you, what you bring forth will save you. If you do not bring forth what is within you, what you do not bring forth will destroy you."

Institutional Threat:

This makes salvation an internal process rather than something mediated by religious institutions.

Saying 113: The Present Kingdom

Saying 113

"The kingdom of the father is spread out upon the earth, and people do not see it."

What We've Been Taught to Miss:

This contradicts the idea of a future heavenly kingdom and suggests enlightenment is available here and now.

Direct Knowledge vs. Blind Faith

Gnosis: Direct Experience

Thomas emphasizes gnosis - direct spiritual knowledge through personal experience, not belief in doctrines.

Saying 5: "Know what is in front of your face, and what is hidden from you will be disclosed."

No Mediators Needed

In Thomas, Jesus opens the door to divinity for all people, eliminating the need for priestly intermediaries.

Saying 108: "Whoever drinks from my mouth will become like me; I myself shall become that person."

The Colombian Parallel

Just as you must experience Colombia's emerald mines personally to understand their beauty, Thomas says you must experience divinity directly to know it.

The Radical Equality in Thomas

Saying 22

"When you make the two one, and when you make the inside like the outside and the outside like the inside, and the above like the below, and when you make the male and the female one and the same... then you will enter the kingdom."

Why This Threatened Power Structures:

This eliminates hierarchies - male/female, clergy/laity, sacred/profane - that religious institutions rely on for control.

Thomas vs. Canonical Gospels: The Differences

Fundamental Differences in Message

Aspect Canonical Gospels Gospel of Thomas
Salvation Through faith in Jesus's death and resurrection Through self-knowledge and spiritual awakening
Jesus's Role Savior to be worshipped Spiritual guide who reveals your own divinity
The Kingdom Future heavenly realm Present reality to be recognized
Path to God Through Church and sacraments Direct personal experience

What Thomas Lacks

No virgin birth, no miracles, no crucifixion, no resurrection, no apocalyptic judgment. These were likely added later to create a religion.

The Political Problem

Thomas's Jesus doesn't threaten Roman authorities - he threatens religious authorities by making them unnecessary.

Power to the People

Every person becomes their own priest, their own mediator with God. This was unacceptable to those building religious empires.
