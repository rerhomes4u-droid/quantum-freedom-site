Equity Principle Acceptance = Payment
1
Call Opening & Information Gathering

When the collector introduces themselves, respond kindly and gather complete information:

"Well, thank you so much for calling. Who am I speaking with?"
Collector gives their name
"Do you have an employee ID number? What's the company you work for? What's your mailing address? May I have your supervisor's name?"
NEW: "And what is the specific reference or account number for the commercial instrument you're inquiring about?"
Why this works: Grounds the call in the specific "offer" or "instrument" you need to discharge. Essential for creating a proper record.
2
Ask What They Need & Make Equity Request

Once you have all information, ask what they need and request proper written presentment:

"Great. So, what is it you're needing from me today?"
Collector states a demand for payment
"Thank you. For this to be settled equitably, I require a proper written presentment of the claim. If you put that in writing and mail it to me, I will honor it immediately upon receipt. Could you do that for me?"
3
Handle Objections with "Acceptance = Payment"

If the collector insists you haven't paid, use the core equity principle:

"You haven't made payments on this account."
"Does your record reflect that I have accepted and returned all presentments sent to me for the settlement and closure of this matter?"
"No, you haven't." or continued arguing
NEW EQUITY SCRIPT: "That is the point of your call. A presentment requires my acceptance. I am offering to accept and honor your presentment, but I require it in proper written form to do so equitably."
Mindset Shift: This embodies the key eureka moment from class. You're shifting from "hoping it works" to "knowing equity prevails." (As Barb explained in the chat).
4
Ending the Call Firmly & Kindly

Based on their response, end the call appropriately:

If they agree to send written presentment:
"Thank you. I will watch for your mailing. Have a good day."
NEW: If they refuse to send written notice:
"Since you're unable to provide the required commercial presentment in writing, there is nothing further I can honor on this call. Please note my request for the record. Thank you for your time."
Staying on Script: This helps users "stay on script regardless of what they say," as Richard advised, and protects against doubt.

Legal Principles Applied

E
Principle of Equity
Equity seeks justice based on fairness principles, not just technical rules. By offering to "honor" the debt with proper presentment, you're operating under equitable principles that supersede mere commercial demands.
U
UCC 1-308 Reservation of Rights
This UCC provision allows you to reserve all rights while still proceeding. Always include "Without Prejudice UCC 1-308" in your written communications to maintain your position while engaging.
W
Written Presentment Requirement
Commercial law requires proper presentment of claims. By requesting written documentation, you ensure a formal process and create a record. "Acceptance = Payment" can only happen with a proper instrument to accept.
M
Maxim: "That which is equitable and good is the law of laws"
This foundational maxim from Burton's establishes that equitable principles are supreme. When you operate from equity, you are invoking the highest law, not merely commercial codes.
